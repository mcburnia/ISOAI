# AI System Inventory Standard

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the requirements for maintaining an inventory of artificial intelligence systems operated or governed by the organisation.

The AI System Inventory provides visibility over the AI systems within the scope of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Maintaining a structured inventory ensures that the organisation understands where AI technologies are being used, who is responsible for them, and what governance controls apply.

The inventory forms a core governance component of the Artificial Intelligence Management System established in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

The AI System Inventory includes artificial intelligence systems that are:

- developed internally
- deployed within analytical or automation platforms
- used as part of consulting engagements
- used to support research, modelling, or analytical work
- integrated into operational workflows

The inventory must include systems across all environments including development, testing, and production where those systems influence professional work or operational processes.

---

## 3. Principles of the AI System Inventory

The AI System Inventory operates according to several governance principles.

### Visibility

The organisation must maintain visibility over all AI systems within scope.

Systems must not operate outside governance oversight.

---

### Accountability

Each system must have a clearly identified owner responsible for its operation and oversight.

Ownership ensures that governance responsibilities are not ambiguous.

---

### Traceability

Information recorded in the inventory must allow systems to be traced through their lifecycle, including deployment approvals and risk assessments.

---

### Proportionality

The level of detail recorded in the inventory should remain proportionate to the scope and impact of the system.

Systems with higher operational or regulatory impact may require more detailed governance records.

---

## 4. Required Inventory Information

Each AI system recorded in the inventory must include the following information.

### System Name

The name used to identify the system within the organisation.

---

### System Description

A short description explaining the purpose of the system and the function it performs.

---

### System Owner

The individual responsible for the operation and oversight of the system.

This individual is typically the AI System Owner defined within the AI Roles and Responsibilities framework.

---

### Operational Environment

The environment in which the system operates.

Examples may include:

- research
- development
- internal analysis
- production deployment
- consulting engagement support

---

### System Type

The type of AI system or technology involved.

Examples include:

- machine learning model
- large language model application
- automated analytical tool
- decision support system
- agent-based automation

---

### Risk Classification

The assessed risk level of the system according to the AI Risk Management Framework.

Typical classifications may include:

- Low
- Medium
- High

---

### Human Oversight Arrangement

A description of the human oversight mechanisms applied to the system.

This may include review procedures, operational reviewers, or other safeguards.

---

### Deployment Status

The current operational status of the system.

Typical values may include:

- Development
- Testing
- Active
- Suspended
- Retired

---

## 5. Inventory Structure

The AI System Inventory should be maintained in a structured format that allows systems to be easily reviewed.

The following fields represent the minimum recommended structure.

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| System Name | Name used to identify the system |
| System Description | Short description of system purpose |
| System Owner | Responsible individual |
| Operational Environment | Environment in which the system operates |
| System Type | Category of AI system |
| Risk Classification | Risk level according to the AI Risk Management Framework |
| Human Oversight | Description of oversight arrangements |
| Deployment Status | Current operational status |
| Last Review Date | Date of most recent governance review |

---

## 6. Maintenance of the Inventory

The AI System Inventory must be maintained as a living governance document.

Entries must be created when:

- a new AI system is introduced
- an existing system changes scope or functionality
- a system progresses through lifecycle stages
- a system is retired

The inventory should be reviewed periodically as part of governance activities.

---

## 7. Relationship to Other Governance Processes

The AI System Inventory supports other governance processes within the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Information recorded in the inventory may reference:

- AI Risk Register entries
- lifecycle approval records
- monitoring and operational review records
- incident investigation records

The inventory provides a central reference point for these governance artefacts.

---
