# AI Monitoring and Performance Review Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the procedure used by the organisation to monitor the behaviour and performance of artificial intelligence systems during operation.

Artificial intelligence systems may change behaviour over time due to changes in data, operational context, or system configuration. Monitoring ensures that such changes are detected and that systems continue to operate as intended.

The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that AI systems remain reliable, appropriate, and aligned with their intended use throughout their operational life.

This procedure forms part of the Artificial Intelligence Management System established in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to artificial intelligence systems within the scope of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Monitoring activities apply to systems operating in:

- analytical environments
- automated workflows
- research and modelling activities
- operational processes supported by AI systems

The level of monitoring should remain proportionate to the potential impact of the system.

---

## 3. Monitoring Objectives

Monitoring activities support several governance objectives.

These include:

- ensuring that systems continue to produce appropriate outputs
- identifying unexpected behaviour
- detecting performance degradation
- identifying changes in operational conditions that may affect system reliability

Monitoring supports responsible use of AI systems and ensures that issues are identified early.

---

## 4. Monitoring Activities

Monitoring may involve several types of activity.

### Output Review

Reviewing system outputs to confirm that results remain appropriate for their intended purpose.

Human reviewers may identify unusual or unreliable outputs during routine use.

---

### Operational Observation

Observing system behaviour during operation to identify unexpected patterns or anomalies.

This may include identifying unusual responses or operational irregularities.

---

### Data Environment Changes

Changes in data sources or data characteristics may influence system behaviour.

Monitoring activities should consider whether data inputs have changed significantly.

---

### Usage Context Changes

Systems may be used in contexts that differ from their original design.

Monitoring should consider whether the operational context of the system remains appropriate.

---

## 5. Responsibilities

Monitoring responsibilities are distributed across several roles.

The AI System Owner is responsible for ensuring that monitoring activities occur for the systems they oversee.

Operational reviewers may identify unusual outputs during normal use.

The AI Governance Lead may initiate periodic review of systems where governance concerns arise.

---

## 6. Monitoring Frequency

Monitoring frequency should remain proportionate to system impact.

Systems with higher operational or regulatory impact may require more frequent review.

Lower impact analytical tools may be reviewed less frequently.

Monitoring may occur through:

- routine use of the system
- periodic governance review
- operational observation by system owners

---

## 7. Performance Concerns

If monitoring activities identify potential issues with system behaviour, the matter should be reviewed.

Potential concerns may include:

- declining reliability of outputs
- unusual system behaviour
- outputs that appear inconsistent with expected results

Where concerns are confirmed, the issue may be escalated under the AI Incident Management Procedure.

---

## 8. Documentation of Monitoring

Monitoring activities may be documented where appropriate.

Documentation may include:

- notes regarding observed behaviour
- records of periodic review
- observations of potential system issues

These records support transparency within the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

---

## 9. Periodic Review

AI systems should be reviewed periodically to determine whether they remain appropriate for their intended purpose.

Periodic review may consider:

- operational experience with the system
- changes in regulatory expectations
- lessons identified from incidents
- changes in system usage patterns

Periodic review ensures that systems remain aligned with organisational governance expectations.

---

## 10. Ownership and Review

Document Owner: AI Governance Lead  
Review Cycle: Annual  
Approval Authority: Senior Management
