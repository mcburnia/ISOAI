# AI Roles and Responsibilities

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the organisational roles and responsibilities associated with the governance and oversight of artificial intelligence systems within Gibbs Consulting.

Clear accountability is required to ensure that AI systems are designed, deployed, and operated in a controlled and professionally responsible manner.

This document supports the Artificial Intelligence Management System and establishes the governance structure required for effective oversight of AI activity.

---

## 2. Governance Principles

AI governance within Gibbs Consulting follows several core principles.

Accountability must be explicit. Each AI system must have an identified owner responsible for its operation and oversight.

Professional judgement must remain central to all analytical activity. Automated systems assist professional work but do not replace human accountability.

Governance must remain proportionate to the size and operational scope of the organisation while remaining credible to regulators and professional stakeholders.

---

## 3. Governance Structure

AI governance within the organisation operates through a small number of clearly defined roles.

These roles collectively provide oversight across the AI system lifecycle.

The primary governance roles are:

- AI Governance Lead
- Technical Architecture Lead
- Data Governance Lead
- AI System Owner
- Operational Reviewer

Depending on the scope of a particular system or engagement, one individual may perform multiple roles provided that accountability remains clear.

---

## 4. AI Governance Lead

The AI Governance Lead holds overall responsibility for the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

This role is responsible for ensuring that AI systems are governed in accordance with organisational policy and professional obligations.

Responsibilities include:

- maintaining the Artificial Intelligence Governance Policy
- overseeing operation of the Artificial Intelligence Management System
- reviewing significant AI risks
- approving high impact AI deployments
- overseeing incident response where AI systems behave unexpectedly
- ensuring that governance documentation remains current

The AI Governance Lead acts as the central authority for AI governance within the organisation.

---

## 5. Technical Architecture Lead

The Technical Architecture Lead is responsible for the design integrity of AI systems.

This role ensures that AI systems are implemented in accordance with architectural standards and that technical controls exist to support traceability, explainability, and operational reliability.

Responsibilities include:

- reviewing AI system architecture before deployment
- ensuring alignment with architectural principles and standards
- confirming that appropriate audit and logging capabilities exist
- verifying that data lineage and system traceability are maintained
- advising on technical mitigation of AI risks

The Technical Architecture Lead provides independent architectural oversight of AI system design.

---

## 6. Data Governance Lead

The Data Governance Lead ensures that data used by AI systems is appropriately governed.

AI systems depend heavily on the quality, provenance, and classification of the data they consume. This role ensures that appropriate controls exist.

Responsibilities include:

- overseeing data classification and access controls
- ensuring that data lineage can be traced
- reviewing data quality considerations for AI systems
- advising on appropriate data retention and usage controls
- ensuring alignment with data governance standards

This role supports the responsible and auditable use of data within AI systems.

---

## 7. AI System Owner

Every AI system must have a named owner.

The AI System Owner is responsible for the safe and effective operation of the system.

Responsibilities include:

- defining the purpose and scope of the system
- ensuring the system is registered in the AI system inventory
- ensuring risks are assessed prior to deployment
- monitoring system performance
- escalating issues or incidents when necessary
- ensuring that human oversight arrangements are defined

The AI System Owner retains operational accountability for the system.

---

## 8. Operational Reviewer

Where AI systems support analytical work or automated processes, outputs must remain subject to professional review where appropriate.

Operational reviewers are responsible for assessing the outputs of AI systems before those outputs are relied upon for professional work or client deliverables.

Responsibilities may include:

- reviewing automated analytical outputs
- validating the appropriateness of conclusions generated by AI tools
- identifying unexpected behaviour or anomalies
- escalating concerns where outputs appear unreliable

Operational reviewers act as a safeguard against inappropriate reliance on automated outputs.

---

## 9. Separation of Responsibilities

Where practical, system design, operational ownership, and oversight should not be concentrated in a single role.

For smaller projects this separation may be limited, but the organisation should maintain awareness of potential conflicts of responsibility.

Where roles overlap, governance decisions should be documented to preserve transparency.

---

## 10. Review of Responsibilities

The roles defined in this document may evolve as the organisation expands its use of AI technologies.

Responsibilities will be reviewed periodically to ensure that governance arrangements remain appropriate and effective.
