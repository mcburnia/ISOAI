# Human Oversight Framework for Artificial Intelligence Systems

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the framework used by Gibbs Consulting to ensure that human oversight remains central to the operation of artificial intelligence systems.

Artificial intelligence systems can assist analytical and operational tasks. They cannot assume responsibility for professional judgement or organisational accountability.

The purpose of this framework is to ensure that automated systems operate under clearly defined human authority and that outputs produced by AI systems remain subject to professional scrutiny.

This framework forms part of the Artificial Intelligence Management System established in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

This framework applies to artificial intelligence systems that influence analytical work, automated processes, or advisory outputs produced by Gibbs Consulting.

Human oversight requirements apply to systems that:

- generate analytical summaries or recommendations
- support research or advisory activities
- assist operational decision processes
- automate analytical workflows
- generate outputs that may be relied upon in professional work

The level of oversight required should remain proportionate to the potential impact of the system.

---

## 3. Principles of Human Oversight

Human oversight of AI systems is governed by several core principles.

### Professional Accountability

Responsibility for decisions remains with qualified professionals.

AI systems may support analysis but do not replace professional judgement.

---

### Qualified Review

Where AI systems produce outputs that influence advisory work or operational decisions, those outputs must be reviewed by an appropriately qualified individual.

The reviewer must have sufficient domain knowledge to evaluate the reliability and appropriateness of the output.

---

### Authority to Intervene

Human reviewers must retain the authority to challenge or override automated outputs.

Automated systems must not prevent human intervention where concerns arise regarding the accuracy or appropriateness of outputs.

---

### Transparency of System Behaviour

Human reviewers must be able to understand the purpose of the system and the context in which outputs are generated.

Systems must retain sufficient records to allow reviewers to understand how outputs were produced.

---

## 4. Qualified-Human-in-the-Loop (QHITL)

Where AI systems influence professional work, oversight should normally follow a Qualified-Human-in-the-Loop model.

Under this model, a qualified reviewer examines outputs before those outputs are relied upon for advisory or analytical work.

The reviewer may:

- confirm the output is appropriate
- modify the output where necessary
- reject the output where it appears unreliable

The QHITL model ensures that professional accountability remains with qualified individuals.

---

## 5. Oversight Triggers

Human review should occur when any of the following conditions apply.

The system produces outputs that may influence:

- advisory recommendations
- regulatory interpretation
- analytical conclusions
- client deliverables
- operational decisions

Human review may also be triggered where:

- outputs appear unusual or inconsistent
- the system operates outside its intended scope
- underlying data sources change significantly

---

## 6. Responsibilities of Human Reviewers

Human reviewers performing oversight activities are responsible for:

- assessing whether outputs are appropriate for the intended use
- identifying signs of unreliable or misleading outputs
- applying professional judgement when interpreting results
- escalating concerns where system behaviour appears problematic

Reviewers must remain aware of the limitations of automated reasoning systems.

---

## 7. Escalation of Concerns

If a reviewer believes that an AI system is producing unreliable outputs, the issue should be escalated.

Escalation may involve:

- notifying the AI System Owner
- requesting additional review by the Technical Architecture Lead
- initiating investigation under the AI Incident Management Procedure

Escalation ensures that potential system issues are investigated promptly.

---

## 8. Documentation of Oversight

Where appropriate, human oversight activities should be documented.

Documentation may include:

- records of output review
- notes regarding modifications to automated outputs
- decisions to override system behaviour
- observations of unusual system behaviour

These records support transparency and accountability within the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

---

## 9. Oversight Limitations

Human oversight does not guarantee that automated outputs will always be correct.

Reviewers must remain alert to the possibility that AI systems may produce plausible but inaccurate outputs.

Professional judgement must therefore remain central to the interpretation of automated outputs.

---

## 10. Ownership and Review

Document Owner: AI Governance Lead  
Review Cycle: Annual  
Approval Authority: Senior Management
