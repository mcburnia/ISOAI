# AI Training and Competence Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the approach used by the organisation to ensure that individuals involved in the design, operation, and use of artificial intelligence systems possess appropriate knowledge and competence.

Artificial intelligence systems introduce technical, operational, and regulatory considerations that require informed oversight. Personnel must understand both the capabilities and the limitations of automated systems.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that staff who interact with AI systems are equipped to use those systems responsibly and to exercise appropriate professional judgement.

This policy forms part of the Artificial Intelligence Management System established in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to individuals who participate in activities involving artificial intelligence systems within the organisation.

This includes individuals who:

- design or develop AI systems
- operate or maintain AI systems
- review outputs produced by AI systems
- use AI tools to support analytical or advisory work

Training expectations may vary depending on the individual’s role and responsibilities.

---

## 3. Principles of AI Competence

The organisation follows several principles when developing competence in relation to AI systems.

### Awareness of AI Capabilities and Limitations

Individuals must understand the capabilities of AI systems as well as their limitations.

AI systems may generate outputs that appear plausible while containing inaccuracies. Users must remain aware of this possibility.

---

### Responsible Use

AI tools must be used in ways that support professional work rather than replace professional judgement.

Users must understand when human review is required and must avoid relying on automated outputs without appropriate scrutiny.

---

### Governance Awareness

Individuals involved in AI systems must understand the governance procedures that apply to those systems.

This includes awareness of:

- AI risk management processes
- human oversight requirements
- incident reporting procedures
- monitoring responsibilities

---

## 4. Training Expectations

Training should ensure that relevant personnel understand the governance framework surrounding AI systems.

Training may include topics such as:

- responsible use of AI tools
- limitations of automated reasoning systems
- governance procedures within the Artificial Intelligence Management System
- human oversight responsibilities
- identification and reporting of AI incidents

Training may be delivered through internal briefings, documentation, or structured learning sessions.

---

## 5. Competence for Key Roles

Individuals performing governance roles within the Artificial Intelligence Management System should possess appropriate knowledge for their responsibilities.

Examples include:

AI Governance Lead  
Understanding of governance frameworks, regulatory considerations, and organisational AI policies.

Technical Architecture Lead  
Understanding of system design principles, data governance, and operational reliability.

AI System Owner  
Understanding of system operation, monitoring responsibilities, and risk management.

Operational Reviewer  
Understanding of how to interpret system outputs and identify potential reliability concerns.

---

## 6. Continuous Learning

Artificial intelligence technologies evolve rapidly. Individuals involved in AI systems should maintain awareness of developments that may affect system behaviour or governance expectations.

Continuous learning may include reviewing emerging regulatory guidance, technical developments, and lessons learned from operational experience.

---

## 7. Documentation of Training

Where appropriate, training activities may be recorded to demonstrate that individuals have received guidance regarding responsible AI use.

Documentation may include:

- records of training sessions
- acknowledgement of governance policies
- evidence of participation in learning activities

These records may support internal governance review.

---

## 8. Ownership and Review

Document Owner: AI Governance Lead  
Review Cycle: Annual  
Approval Authority: Senior Management
