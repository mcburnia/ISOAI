# AI Risk Register

## 1. Purpose

The AI Risk Register records risks associated with artificial intelligence systems operated or governed by Gibbs Consulting.

The register provides visibility over identified risks, mitigation actions, and responsible owners. It allows the organisation to monitor AI risk exposure across all systems within the scope of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

The register supports the AI Risk Management Framework and provides operational evidence that AI risks are being managed in a structured and auditable manner.

---

## 2. Scope

The AI Risk Register includes risks identified during:

- development of AI systems
- deployment approval processes
- operational monitoring
- incident investigation
- periodic governance review

Each AI system within the AI system inventory may generate one or more entries in the register.

---

## 3. Risk Identification

Each risk recorded in the register must include a clear description of the potential issue.

The description should explain:

- what could go wrong
- how the issue might arise
- what the potential consequences could be

Descriptions should be specific enough to allow meaningful review and mitigation planning.

---

## 4. Risk Assessment

Each risk must be assessed using two dimensions.

### Likelihood

The probability that the risk may occur.

Typical ratings may include:

- Low
- Medium
- High

---

### Impact

The potential severity of consequences if the risk occurs.

Impact should consider:

- operational disruption
- incorrect analysis or recommendations
- regulatory exposure
- reputational damage

---

## 5. Risk Mitigation

Where risks exceed acceptable levels, mitigation actions must be defined.

Mitigation measures may include:

- additional technical controls
- improved monitoring
- introduction of human review
- data quality improvements
- limitation of system scope

Mitigation actions must have a named owner responsible for implementation.

---

## 6. Risk Ownership

Each risk must have a clearly identified owner.

The owner is responsible for:

- monitoring the risk
- implementing mitigation actions
- reporting changes in risk status
- ensuring periodic review

Ownership typically rests with the relevant AI System Owner.

---

## 7. Risk Review

Risks recorded in the register must be reviewed periodically.

Review may be triggered by:

- significant system changes
- operational incidents
- periodic governance review
- changes in regulatory expectations

Reviews ensure that risks remain accurately classified and mitigation measures remain effective.

---

## 8. Risk Register Structure

The following fields form the minimum structure of the AI Risk Register.

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Risk ID | Unique identifier for the risk |
| System Name | AI system associated with the risk |
| Risk Description | Clear description of the risk |
| Risk Category | Regulatory, operational, data, bias, security, or misuse |
| Likelihood | Probability of occurrence |
| Impact | Severity of consequences |
| Risk Rating | Combined assessment of likelihood and impact |
| Mitigation Measures | Actions defined to reduce the risk |
| Risk Owner | Responsible individual |
| Review Date | Date of next scheduled review |
| Status | Open, Mitigated, Closed |

## 10. Maintenance of the Register

The AI Risk Register is maintained as a living document.

Entries may be added or updated whenever:

- a new AI system is introduced
- new risks are identified
- mitigation actions are implemented
- risk status changes

The register should be reviewed periodically as part of AI governance activities.
