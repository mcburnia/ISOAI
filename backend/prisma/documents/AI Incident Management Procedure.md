# AI Incident Management Procedure

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the procedure used by Gibbs Consulting to identify, investigate, and respond to incidents involving artificial intelligence systems.

AI systems may occasionally produce unexpected or unreliable outputs. When this occurs, the organisation must be able to investigate the issue and take appropriate corrective action.

The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that AI-related incidents are handled in a structured and auditable manner.

This procedure forms part of the Artificial Intelligence Management System established in alignment with ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to incidents involving artificial intelligence systems within the scope of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Incidents may arise during:

- development or testing
- operational use of AI systems
- analytical work supported by automated tools
- automated processes or workflows

The procedure applies to incidents identified by system operators, reviewers, or monitoring activities.

---

## 3. Definition of an AI Incident

An AI incident occurs when an artificial intelligence system behaves in a manner that could lead to incorrect, misleading, or inappropriate outcomes.

Examples of incidents may include:

- inaccurate or misleading automated outputs
- biased or unfair results
- unexpected system behaviour
- misuse of data within a system
- operational disruption caused by AI system behaviour

Not every error constitutes a formal incident. Incidents should be recorded where the issue could affect professional work, operational processes, or regulatory obligations.

---

## 4. Incident Identification

AI incidents may be identified through several channels.

These include:

- human reviewers identifying unusual outputs
- operational monitoring activities
- system owners identifying unexpected behaviour
- feedback from users or stakeholders

Where a potential incident is identified, it should be reported to the AI System Owner.

---

## 5. Initial Assessment

Once reported, the AI System Owner performs an initial assessment.

This assessment should determine:

- whether the issue represents a genuine incident
- the potential impact of the issue
- whether immediate mitigation is required

If the issue appears significant, the AI Governance Lead should be informed.

---

## 6. Incident Investigation

Where an incident is confirmed, an investigation should be conducted.

The purpose of the investigation is to determine:

- what happened
- why the issue occurred
- whether similar issues could occur again

Investigation activities may include:

- reviewing system logs
- reviewing decision records or outputs
- examining underlying data sources
- assessing system configuration

The Technical Architecture Lead may assist in technical investigation where required.

---

## 7. Corrective Actions

Where an investigation identifies the cause of an incident, corrective actions should be defined.

Examples of corrective actions may include:

- adjustments to system configuration
- additional human oversight
- improvements to data quality controls
- modification of system usage guidance
- temporary suspension of system use

Corrective actions should be recorded and tracked until completed.

---

## 8. Escalation

Where an incident involves significant operational, reputational, or regulatory risk, escalation may be required.

Escalation may involve:

- notifying senior leadership
- suspending the affected system
- conducting additional governance review

Escalation ensures that significant issues receive appropriate attention.

---

## 9. Documentation of Incidents

All confirmed incidents should be documented.

Documentation should include:

- description of the incident
- date of occurrence
- systems involved
- investigation findings
- corrective actions taken

Incident records provide evidence that governance procedures are functioning as intended.

---

## 10. Learning and Improvement

Incidents provide opportunities to improve governance practices.

Following an incident investigation, the organisation should consider whether improvements are required in areas such as:

- system design
- operational monitoring
- governance procedures
- user guidance

Lessons learned should be incorporated into future governance practices where appropriate.

---

## 11. Ownership and Review

Document Owner: AI Governance Lead  
Review Cycle: Annual  
Approval Authority: Senior Management
