# Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) Manual

## 1. Purpose

This document describes the structure and operation of the Artificial Intelligence Management System operated by Gibbs Consulting.

The management system establishes governance, oversight, and operational controls for the responsible design, deployment, and use of artificial intelligence systems.

The system ensures that AI activity within the organisation is conducted in a controlled, auditable, and professionally responsible manner.

The Artificial Intelligence Management System aligns with the requirements of ISO/IEC 42001.

---

## 2. Scope

The Artificial Intelligence Management System applies to artificial intelligence systems that are:

- designed or developed by Gibbs Consulting
- operated within internal analytical or automation platforms
- deployed as part of consulting engagements
- used to support research, modelling, analysis, or automated workflows

Artificial intelligence systems include, but are not limited to:

- machine learning models
- large language model applications
- automated decision support tools
- agent-based automation
- predictive analytics models
- AI-assisted research and analysis tools

The system governs activity across all environments including development, testing, research, and production.

---

## 3. Organisational Context

Gibbs Consulting operates in professional and regulated environments where analytical integrity, traceability, and professional accountability are essential.

Artificial intelligence technologies are capable of assisting complex analytical and operational tasks. These technologies also introduce new forms of risk that must be governed appropriately.

The Artificial Intelligence Management System ensures that AI technologies are used in a controlled manner that preserves professional accountability and supports regulatory expectations for transparency, explainability, and auditability.

---

## 4. Structure of the AI Management System

The Artificial Intelligence Management System is composed of several integrated governance components.

These components provide oversight across the lifecycle of AI systems.

### **Governance**

Defines the policies, roles, and organisational responsibilities that govern the use of AI within the organisation.

### **Risk Management**

Defines how AI-related risks are identified, assessed, and mitigated.

### **Lifecycle Governance**

Defines how AI systems are approved, developed, deployed, monitored, and retired.

### **Human Oversight**

Defines how human judgement is retained in automated decision environments.

### **Operational Monitoring**

Defines how AI system performance, behaviour, and outcomes are monitored.

### **Incident Management**

Defines how unexpected or harmful AI behaviour is identified and addressed.

### **Continuous Improvement**

Defines how the management system evolves through review, audit, and organisational learning.

---

## 5. AI System Lifecycle Governance

AI systems are governed throughout their operational lifecycle.

Each system must progress through defined governance stages before deployment.

Typical lifecycle stages include:

- concept and initial assessment
- design and architecture review
- risk assessment
- development and testing
- approval for deployment
- operational monitoring
- review or retirement

Approval checkpoints exist at key lifecycle stages to ensure that risks have been considered and appropriate oversight mechanisms are in place.

---

## 6. AI Risk Management

All AI systems must be assessed for potential risks before they are deployed.

Risk assessment considers factors such as:

- potential regulatory impact
- operational safety
- reliance on automated outputs
- fairness and bias
- misuse or unintended behaviour

Risks are recorded in the organisational AI risk register and reviewed periodically.

Mitigation actions are defined where risks exceed acceptable thresholds.

---

## 7. AI System Inventory

The organisation maintains a register of AI systems that fall within the scope of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

The system inventory records essential governance information including:

- system purpose
- system owner
- operational environment
- risk classification
- human oversight arrangements

The inventory allows the organisation to maintain visibility over all AI systems in operation.

---

## 8. Human Oversight

AI systems must operate within defined human oversight arrangements.

Where automated systems influence analytical outcomes or operational decisions, human reviewers must retain the authority to:

- review automated outputs
- challenge automated conclusions
- override automated actions where appropriate

Human oversight arrangements must be defined before deployment of AI systems.

---

## 9. Monitoring and Performance Review

AI systems must be monitored to ensure they continue to operate as intended.

Monitoring activities may include:

- reviewing operational performance
- identifying unexpected behaviour
- detecting drift or degradation in model performance
- reviewing audit logs and decision records

Monitoring results may trigger review or remediation actions.

---

## 10. Incident Management

Unexpected or harmful behaviour in AI systems must be addressed through defined incident management procedures.

Examples of incidents may include:

- incorrect or misleading automated outputs
- bias or unfair outcomes
- operational failures
- unexpected behaviour arising from model drift or environmental change

Incidents are investigated and corrective actions are defined where necessary.

---

## 11. Documentation and Records

The Artificial Intelligence Management System is supported by documented policies, procedures, and operational records.

These documents provide evidence that governance processes are being followed.

Records may include:

- risk assessments
- system approvals
- decision logs
- monitoring results
- incident investigations

Documentation must be retained in accordance with organisational retention policies.

---

## 12. Internal Review and Improvement

The Artificial Intelligence Management System is subject to periodic review.

Reviews consider:

- operational experience with AI systems
- changes in regulatory expectations
- incidents or operational issues
- opportunities for governance improvement

Findings from internal review may result in updates to policies, procedures, or governance practices.

---

## 13. Relationship to Supporting Standards

The Artificial Intelligence Management System is supported by a number of detailed governance standards and technical documents.

These documents define operational practices in areas such as:

- AI architecture and system design
- audit trails and decision logging
- data governance and classification
- data quality management
- operational cost governance for AI systems

These standards provide the technical controls that support the governance framework defined in this manual.
